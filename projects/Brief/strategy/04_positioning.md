# 04 — Positioning

## The positioning sentence

> **"Every AI tool asks you to explain your business. Brief is the one-time interview that gives Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, and every other AI tool the context they need to actually sound like you."**

This sentence does three things:

1. **Names the pain** in the first clause (every AI tool asks you to explain your business).
2. **Promises a one-time fix** (not another subscription to manage).
3. **Lists the tools by name** (immediate resonance — the user recognizes their own stack).

## Category

**Category: "AI Onboarding Tool"** — not "brand voice tool," not "AI content platform," not "knowledge management."

This is a deliberate choice. We are positioning Brief as *the thing you use when you start* with AI, not *another thing to add to your AI stack.* The word "onboarding" frames the product as a one-time action with a clear end state, which is exactly what one-time pricing should feel like.

If "AI onboarding tool" doesn't land in tests, fallback category: **"AI Brief / Context Primer."**

### The compounding narrative (V1 → V2 arc)

V1 positioning is "Brief it once." But the full story arc — visible from launch, delivered in V2 — is **"Brief it once. Let it compound."**

This draws directly from Andrej Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern (April 2026): instead of re-deriving context from raw sources every time, build a persistent, structured, interlinked knowledge base that the LLM maintains and improves over time. Brief is a concrete implementation of that pattern for company context.

V1 produces the initial compilation. V2 adds the maintenance operations — ingest new sources (new case study, updated pricing, fresh testimonial), query the wiki ("what's our current positioning against Competitor X?"), and lint for contradictions ("your pricing page changed but your Brief still says the old price"). The Brief becomes a compounding asset, not a static document.

This narrative also addresses the #1 objection to one-time purchases: "but my business changes." Yes, and Brief grows with it — that's V2. The one-time V1 gets you started; the subscription V2 keeps you current.

**Cite Karpathy explicitly in marketing.** "Built on the LLM Wiki pattern described by Andrej Karpathy" is a credibility signal the technical ICP recognizes instantly. It positions Brief as a serious implementation of a proven architecture, not "another AI SaaS tool."

## Point of view

**The industry believes** you should write better prompts to get better AI output.

**We believe** the prompt isn't the problem. The context is. And the context can't be fixed one prompt at a time — it has to be captured once, correctly, and installed into every AI tool a user owns.

**The implication:** users who keep writing new system prompts are solving the problem the wrong way. Brief replaces that entire loop with a one-time 20-minute interview and a single portable artifact.

## Differentiators (value wedges)

1. **Upstream, not sideways.** Brief is the thing you do *before* using any AI content tool. We don't compete with Jasper or Writer — we feed them.
2. **Platform-neutral by design.** Export to Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Custom GPT, Markdown, raw JSON. No lock-in. No walled garden. Works with whatever AI tool the user loves.
3. **The interview, not the form.** We don't just give you a blank schema to fill out. We interview you — with intelligent follow-ups, positioning-expert-crafted questions, and context inference from your website. This is the hardest part to copy and the most valuable part to the user.
4. **One-time purchase, no subscription.** Honest pricing for a product that delivers value once.
5. **Built by an AI ops agency.** The people who made Brief also build the AI ops you'll want next. Brief is opinionated because its creators run production AI stacks for a living.
6. **Wiki-native architecture.** Brief doesn't produce a flat document — it produces an interlinked wiki (following Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern) with frontmatter, cross-references, and an index. Opens natively in Obsidian. This means it compounds over time instead of going stale in a forgotten Google Doc.

## Naming rationale

**Why "Brief":**
- One syllable, memorable, unambiguous
- Double meaning: a brief (noun, the document) and to brief (verb, the action of preparing someone)
- Agency-world vocabulary — signals professionalism and implies "we know what we're doing"
- Extensible: Brief Pro, Brief Wiki (V2), Brief for Agencies
- Domain availability: brief.secondteam.ai works immediately; standalone domain brief.ai is likely taken but brief.io / usebrief.com / getbrief.com are testable

**Names we considered and rejected:**
- **Context** — too generic, category-crowded
- **Primer** — good but less brandable
- **Groundwork** — too heavy
- **Keystone** — too architectural
- **MCCL (Master Company Context Layer)** — engineering-brain, scary to beginners
- **The Brief** — cleaner than "Brief" but harder to search for

## Brand voice for Brief (meta note)

The product itself should feel:
- **Confident, not apologetic.** We know what belongs in a company brief because we do this for a living.
- **Warm, not corporate.** The user is overwhelmed; we're the friend who's been through it.
- **Concrete, not abstract.** Every question has an example answer; every section has a "why this matters."
- **Opinionated, not neutral.** We tell users what good looks like. Neutrality is unhelpful.
- **Fast, not thorough.** The interview values completion over comprehensiveness. A done brief beats a perfect one.

## How Brief positions Second Team

This is the second-order benefit of Brief and it might be more valuable than the product revenue.

Before Brief, Second Team is "a software dev agency that also does AI ops." After Brief launches, Second Team becomes **"the agency that builds what Brief gets you ready for."**

That's a cleaner market position. It turns Second Team into the obvious next step for anyone who finished a brief and thought "okay, now what?" And it makes Second Team stand out in a crowded "AI consultancy" space because we have a free, useful top-of-funnel product — very few agencies do.

## Messaging hierarchy

**Primary message:** Stop explaining your business to ChatGPT. Brief it once. Let it compound.

**Pillar 1 — Speed:** From 0 to a usable AI context in 20 minutes, not 20 hours.
- Proof: 10-question free interview, auto-scrape, concierge benchmark
- Example headline: "Twenty minutes. Every AI tool sounds like you."

**Pillar 2 — Portability:** Works with every AI tool, not just one.
- Proof: Export formats for Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Custom GPT, Obsidian vault, Markdown
- Example headline: "Write it once, use it everywhere."

**Pillar 3 — Compounds over time:** Brief isn't a static doc that goes stale — it's a living wiki that grows with your business.
- Proof: Wiki-native structure (Karpathy LLM Wiki pattern), Obsidian-compatible, V2 adds ingest/lint/query operations
- Example headline: "Your context gets smarter every month."

**Pillar 4 — Quality:** The interview is built by the people who run AI ops for a living.
- Proof: Second Team is the creator; interview is informed by Dunford/Christensen/Osterwalder frameworks
- Example headline: "The brief is built by people who use it every day."
