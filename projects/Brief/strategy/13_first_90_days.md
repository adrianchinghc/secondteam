## Week 2 (April 20–26) — First 5 concierge briefs (free)

**Goal:** Deliver 5 briefs manually to founders from Adrian's and Jayson's networks. Collect feedback.

- [ ] Recruit 5 founders from warm network (LinkedIn, Second Team existing clients, founder communities)
- [ ] Conduct 60–90 min interviews via Zoom (record with consent)
- [ ] Draft briefs manually using Claude + Notion template
- [ ] Deliver each brief within 48 hours via email
- [ ] Follow up 3 days later: "Did you use it? Did it change your AI outputs?"
- [ ] Document what worked and what didn't in a running learnings doc

**Deliverable:** 5 delivered briefs + qualitative feedback + first version of the interview refinement notes.

**Key signals to watch for:**
- How long did the interview actually take?
- Which questions produced the most useful answers?
- Which questions produced bad/thin answers?
- Did the brief feel complete, or were they asking for more?
- Did anyone ask about Second Team services unprompted?

## The time cost summary

| Phase | Hours/week | Total hours |
|---|---|---|
| Week 0 alignment | 5 | 5 |
| Weeks 1–4 concierge | 15–20 | 60–80 |
| Weeks 5–12 build | 15–25 (or hire help) | 120–200 |
| Weeks 13–14 launch | 30–40 | 60–80 |
| Weeks 15–18 optimize | 10–15 | 40–60 |
| **Total over 90 days** | — | **~285–425 hours** |

This is a real commitment. Jayson needs to understand that Adrian's Second Team workload will be reduced during this period, or Adrian will burn out.

**Budget this seriously. Don't underestimate it.** The single most common failure mode for side products inside services businesses is the founder underestimating the time cost and then delivering neither well.

## The one sentence to remember

**Ship Brief V1 in July. Generate the first Second Team lead by September. Everything else is negotiable.**

If those two things happen on schedule, Brief is a success. If they don't, we have clear signals for what went wrong.
