## The two gate questions (answer these first)

Everything else downstream depends on these two answers.

### Q1: Strategic fit — does Brief belong inside Second Team?

**Adrian's position:** Yes. Brief is Second Team's top-of-funnel for AI ops services. It qualifies and warms leads at a scale cold outreach can't. It also sharpens Second Team's market positioning from "dev agency that does AI" to "the agency that builds what Brief gets you ready for."

**What we need from Jayson:**
- Do you agree with the strategic framing?
- Or should Brief be structured as a separate entity (different brand, different P&L) to protect Second Team's core focus?
- Or is this the wrong project entirely for Second Team right now?

**Why it matters:** If Brief is standalone, the lead-gen math falls apart and we'd need to build it as a real SaaS — which is a much harder path. If Brief is inside Second Team, the whole plan works. If it's wrong for Second Team now, we stop before wasting time.

---

### Q2: Capacity — can Second Team service Brief's output?

**Adrian's position:** If Brief hits its base-case targets, it produces 3–5 qualified AI ops leads per month by month 6, and 8–12 per month by month 12. Assuming 20–25% close rate, that's 1–3 new services engagements per month.

**What we need from Jayson:**
- Does Second Team have capacity to take on 1–3 new AI ops engagements per month on top of existing work?
- What's the practical ceiling? At what point does Brief start generating more leads than we can close, and what do we do about that?
- Who at Second Team handles the consultation calls? Adrian, Jayson, both, a hired closer?

**Why it matters:** If we build a great funnel into a closed gate, we waste leads and damage the brand. If capacity is the bottleneck, we either delay the launch or plan the hire.

## Product decisions we need to agree on

### Q3: Naming — is "Brief" the right name?

**Adrian's position:** Yes. One syllable, agency-world vocabulary, dual meaning (noun + verb), easy to brand.

**Alternatives worth considering:**
- Primer
- Groundwork
- Context (too crowded)
- The Brief (harder to search)

**What we need:** Your gut check. Does "Brief" feel right to you, or does one of the alternatives fit Second Team's brand voice better?

### Q4: Pricing — is $79 one-time the right number?

**Adrian's position:** $79 one-time. High enough to filter for serious buyers and cover infrastructure, low enough to remove friction.

**Alternatives:**
- $49 (more volume, less filter)
- $99 (more filter, less volume)
- $39 founding price for first 100

**What we need:** Do you agree with $79 as the anchor and test $49 / $99 as A/B variants? Or do you have a stronger read on what this ICP will actually pay?

### Q5: Free tier — is 10 questions enough?

**Adrian's position:** Yes. The free tier should feel valuable but visibly incomplete. 10 questions + scrape + basic exports is enough to deliver a real brief but obvious that the 25-question version would be deeper.

**What we need:** Are you comfortable giving away 10 questions + scrape for free? Some people worry that the free tier gives too much away and kills paid conversion. Others think the free tier should be more generous to drive virality.

### Q6: V2 subscription — are we committed to NOT shipping one in V1?

**Adrian's position:** Yes. Subscription pricing for a static-artifact product is dishonest. We only introduce a subscription (Brief Wiki) in V2, and only if V1 data proves multi-tool pain.

**What we need:** Agreement that we will resist the temptation to add a subscription in V1 just for MRR. Discipline on this is important.

## Operational decisions

### Q7: Who owns Brief?

Brief needs an owner. Options:

- **Adrian as primary owner, Jayson as sponsor/reviewer** — Adrian drives, Jayson advises
- **Jayson as primary owner, Adrian as co-founder** — Jayson drives, Adrian supports
- **Shared ownership** — both make decisions together, which is slower but more aligned
- **Hire an owner** — bring in a part-time operator to drive Brief

**What we need:** A clear owner. Ambiguous ownership kills side projects inside services businesses.

### Q8: How much of Adrian's time goes to Brief?

**Adrian's current estimate:** 10 hours/week during concierge MVP, scaling to ~15 hours/week during V1 build, then ~5 hours/week in steady state post-launch.

**What we need:** Jayson's read on whether that's sustainable alongside Adrian's current Second Team responsibilities. If not, we need to either reduce scope or bring help.

### Q9: Do we hire a part-time engineer for V1?

**Adrian's position:** Probably yes. A good engineer at 20 hours/week for 10 weeks can ship V1. Budget: $15K–$25K one-time for the build. This frees Adrian to focus on the interview design, the positioning, and the early GTM.

**What we need:** Budget approval and agreement that this is worth the spend. Alternative: Adrian builds it solo in ~14–16 weeks instead of 10, which delays launch and increases platform risk.

### Q10: Does Brief deserve its own brand, or lives under Second Team?

**Adrian's position:** Lives under Second Team. "Brief by Second Team" or brief.secondteam.ai. Shared brand reinforces Second Team's positioning.

**Alternative:** Separate brand (brief.io or similar) that could theoretically be spun out later.

**What we need:** Your preference. Shared brand is simpler and reinforces Second Team. Separate brand preserves optionality.

**What we need:** Agreement on the deadline and commitment that we ship on date even if features are 60% of target.

### Q13: What's our kill switch?

**Adrian's proposed kill criteria:** (documented in [10_risks_and_assumptions.md](./10_risks_and_assumptions.md))
- Concierge MVP: fewer than 3 of 10 users say the output meaningfully changed their AI output

- V1 launch: zero Second Team leads generated in 6 months

**What we need:** Agreement on what "this isn't working" looks like — decided now so we don't rationalize failure later.
