## Content-to-Revenue Funnel
The content is NOT explicitly about selling Second Team. The flow is:

1. **Post about solving real problems** in our own businesses using AI — the journey, results, and tools
2. **Audience gets curious** about how we did it → they ask, they follow
3. **On-demand selling:** when they ask "how do I do this?" → point them to the template, course, or tool
4. **Don't announce products in content** — just show the result, let curiosity drive inbound
5. **Community accelerates this loop** — questions in the community reveal exactly what to sell next

*"We don't create content to sell. We create content to build curiosity with what we do."* — Jayson, April 14 L10

## What "Good Content" Looks Like for Second Team
- **Honest** — show failures and pivots, not just wins
- **Specific** — real numbers, real tools, real workflows (not generic "AI tips")
- **Operator-to-operator** — speaking from experience, not from theory
- **Problem-first** — "we had this problem, here's how we solved it" not "here's a feature we built"
- **Consistent** — the cadence matters more than the quality of any single post
- **Fast-moving on news** — react to new AI releases quickly (Jayson's observation: this is what gets views)

## The Content Waterfall (Production SOP)
*Source: Content CEO System — Layer 2. Apply to every pillar piece.*

One YouTube video per week generates the following in sequence:

```
1 YouTube Video (15–30 min — The Build Log)
    ↓
3 Short-form clips  →  IG Reels / TikToks / YT Shorts
3 LinkedIn Posts    →  long-form list, hook stacking, or value-packed image
3 X Threads         →  repurposed from LinkedIn or standalone
    ↓
1 Newsletter issue  →  1 insight + 1 framework + link to YouTube
1 IG Carousel       →  visual breakdown of the key framework from the video
```

Target: ~14–15 assets from one recording session. No original creation for secondary platforms.

**Hook checklist — apply to every piece before publishing:**
- [ ] 3 lines max for the hook
- [ ] 5th-grade reading level, short/kinetic rhythm
- [ ] Uses magic words: I, I've, How
- [ ] Curiosity gap or pattern interrupt in line 1
- [ ] Payoff is clear within 3 seconds

**4 Power Formats rotation (LinkedIn/X) — rotate weekly:**
- Week 1: Long-form List (high engaged time)
- Week 2: Long-form Carousel (scroll depth, PDF format)
- Week 3: Hook Stacking (rhythmic, line-by-line pacing)
- Week 4: Value-Packed Image (hand-drawn visual or diagram as thumbnail)

## The Content GPS Funnel (Audience Ownership)
*Source: Content CEO System — Layer 3. Critical missing piece as of April 2026.*

> "Own your audience, or rent your future." — Owned newsletter converts at 27x the rate of social followers.

The funnel has three layers:

**Top — Rented Audience:** Social platforms (X, LinkedIn, IG, TikTok). High reach, high platform risk. This is where Adrian currently lives entirely.

**Middle — Conversion Mechanism:** CTAs at the end of every carousel and thread trigger DM automation (ManyChat) or drive to a lead magnet. Example CTA: *"Want the full framework? Comment 'SYSTEM' and I'll DM it to you."* This is the bridge between rented and owned.

**Bottom — Owned Audience:** The newsletter (ConvertKit). Weekly cadence. Every subscriber is worth 27x a social follower for revenue conversion. This is the Second Team launch list.

**Build order (by priority):**
1. Set up ConvertKit + simple landing page — do not wait for perfect
2. Create lead magnet: "The AI Replacement Tracker" — Notion template logging hours saved, processes replaced, payroll avoided. Directly tied to The Replacement Log signature format.
3. Set up ManyChat DM automation for IG/LinkedIn carousels
4. Add newsletter link to every platform bio + LinkedIn Featured section
5. Use CEOS video launch (week of Apr 21) as first newsletter acquisition event

## Things to Avoid in Content
- Hype without substance
- Generic "AI is changing everything" takes without operator-level specifics
- Overly polished production (authenticity > production value in Phase 1)
- Explicitly selling in content — let curiosity drive inbound, sell on demand
- Teaching tools you haven't personally used in your own business
- Waiting for an agent or tool to be ready before posting — content cadence is independent of product status
