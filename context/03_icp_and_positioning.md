# ICP, Positioning & Competitive Context
*Last updated: April 11, 2026*

## The ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Core definition:**
Owner-bottlenecked B2B businesses, $2M–$20M revenue, that sell custom or semi-custom products or services to other businesses through relationship-driven sales — where the owner is trapped in daily operations.

### Qualification Filters
| Dimension | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| Revenue | $2M – $20M annual revenue |
| Headcount | 5 – 80 employees |
| Tech Maturity | Low — runs on spreadsheets, WhatsApp/email, fragmented point tools |
| Buyer | The owner/operator (not IT, not a committee) |
| Trigger Pain | Wants to grow without hiring more humans; owner is the bottleneck |
| Model Shape | Custom quotes, relationship sales, B2B buyer, recurring clients |

## Positioning

### Unique Selling Proposition
*"Two operators. Two real businesses. One AI operating system, built live in public — and available when it's ready."*

**Why nobody else can claim this:** Every AI-for-SMB tool on the market is built by engineers who have never run an SMB. We are two operators running two real B2B businesses — a software agency and a B2B distribution company — and we are building Second Team to replace the work we're doing ourselves. You don't have to trust the marketing. You can watch us build it, run it, and break it — in public — every single week.

**The closing line (use after the USP, not as the opener):** "When it's ready to rent, it costs about the same as a part-time employee — but runs 24/7, never quits, and gets smarter every week."

### Messaging Architecture
1. **Lead with:** The two-operators moat — we're running this on our own businesses
2. **Follow with:** What the product does — replaces operational work so owners can focus on building
3. **Close with:** Pricing anchor — cost of a part-time employee

### Positioning Against Competitors

**SMEHero.ai (Malaysia — closest local competitor)**
- Horizontal AI platform for all Malaysian SMEs (HR, finance, legal compliance)
- Backed by Agmo Group, launched late 2025, live product with paying customers
- Pricing: RM160–RM1,600/month (~$35–$340 USD) — significantly cheaper
- Weakness: zero vertical depth. A distributor using SMEHero gets the same product as a restaurant owner
- Our defense: go so deep into B2B distribution operations that SMEHero becomes irrelevant to our ICP
- Their tagline: "The Most Affordable AI in Malaysia" — do NOT compete on price

**Houston (gethouston.ai — US)**
- Open-source, MIT-licensed Mac desktop app targeting US startup founders
- Agents: Growth, Investor Relations, Sales Outreach, Market Research, Data Analyst, Recruiting
- Language/ICP: Silicon Valley (PLG metrics, Series A memos, investor data rooms)
- Not a direct competitor — completely different ICP and vocabulary
- Risk: their "For Founders" white-label framework could be forked by a Malaysian developer

**What no competitor will build:**
- Autocount integration (Malaysian SMB accounting software)
- Distribution-specific workflows (order management, collections follow-up, consignment tracking)
- Bahasa Malaysia support
- Case studies from real Malaysian B2B distributors
- Last-mile onboarding designed for someone who has never touched AI

## The Market Opportunity

**Malaysia / SEA angle (Jayson's vertical):**
- B2B distributors using Autocount = massive, underserved, no modern tooling
- Buyers are 2nd/3rd-gen family business operators — high pain, low tech maturity, local trust required
- No competitor is building this. The niche is open.

**Pro Services / Agency angle (Adrian's vertical):**
- Software dev, design, marketing agencies facing existential AI pressure
- High content/marketing literacy — easier to reach through build-in-public
- Adrian is one = built-in credibility and case study from Day 1

## The Moat
The moat is NOT the agents. Agents will be commoditized.

The real moat is:
1. **Master Company Context Layer** — the structured, living profile of a business that makes every agent smarter over time. Gets more valuable the longer a customer uses it.
2. **Operator Workflow Library** — proprietary, documented understanding of how specific ICPs actually run their businesses. Can't be forked.
3. **Build-in-public audience** — distribution channel that compounds. Takes years to build; can't be bought overnight.
4. **Local trust + cultural proximity** — "built for Malaysian businesses, by people who understand how Malaysian businesses actually run." No American platform can credibly say this.
