# Scorecard & Kill Criteria
*Last updated: April 11, 2026 | Source: V/TO v5*

## The Weekly Scorecard
EOS Scorecard: 5–7 numbers graded every Tuesday at L10. A metric "goes red" when it misses target two weeks in a row — that triggers an Issue (IDS). Scorecard evolves by phase.

### Phase 1–2 (April – June 2026) — Leading Indicators Only
| Metric | Weekly Target | Owner | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------------|-------|----------------|
| Content outputs shipped | Adrian 7 / Jayson 3 (min) | Both | Proves distribution discipline. No posts = no flywheel. |
| Audience net-new (all channels) | +150/week baseline | Adrian | Leading indicator for demand. If this stalls, content isn't landing. |
| Newsletter signups | +25/week | Adrian | Owned channel; conversion leading indicator. |
| Demo / beta interest inbound | ≥5/week by June | Jayson | Market pull signal. If this doesn't show up, ICP is wrong. |
| Beta-partner conversations (discovery) | ≥3/week | Jayson | Forces customer contact discipline even before product exists. |
| Agent-build progress vs. plan | On-track / At-risk / Red | Adrian | Binary check: are the 3 agents on pace? Red = escalate immediately. |
| Founder Sovereign Filter violations | 0 | Both | Zero tolerance. Any yes goes straight to Issues List. |

## Kill Criteria — Pre-Committed Red Lines
*The #1 cause of death for founder-led startups is not the absence of signal — it is the refusal to act on negative signal once sunk cost has accumulated. These are pre-committed. We do not debate whether the line was fair when we hit it — we execute the response.*

| Red Line | Trigger | Pre-Committed Response |
|----------|---------|----------------------|
| Paying subscribers | <25 paying subs by Dec 31, 2026 | Pause all agent development for 90 days. Run full customer-discovery sprint (50+ owner interviews). Re-write ICP and positioning before resuming build. |
| Unit economics | CAC > 0.5 × Year-1 revenue per customer at any point after July 2027 | Halt all paid acquisition. Move back to content-only growth until retention and pricing are fixed. |
| Activation (beta phase) | <40% of beta partners still active at 60 days | Do not launch publicly in December. Extend beta another quarter. Treat the product as not-yet-ready. |
| Churn | Monthly logo churn >8% for 2 consecutive months post-launch | Freeze new feature work. Both founders go on customer calls full-time for 4 weeks to find the root cause. |
| Founder focus | Either founder takes paid consulting or services revenue at any point in Year 1–2 | Immediate L10 emergency session. Either the filter is wrong and we change the V/TO formally, or the revenue is returned and we re-commit. |
| Content discipline | Either founder goes >14 days without posting | Public accountability: the other founder calls it out in the next L10, and we root-cause what broke. No exceptions. |
| Ambition check | We catch ourselves optimizing for a <$30M outcome | Immediate V/TO review. Either re-raise the BHAG or explicitly acknowledge we are building a different company. |

## The October 2026 Stress Test (Year 1.5 Checkpoint)
Before the public launch, pressure-test unit economics. If any two of the four come back red → December launch converts to extended beta. No paid acquisition, no Product Hunt push.

| Question | Target | Kill Threshold |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| Activation — % of beta partners still actively using at 60+ days | ≥70% | <40% |
| Willingness-to-pay — beta partners who convert to $499+/month | ≥3 of 10 | 0 of 10 |
| LTV math — does LTV exceed 3x projected paid CAC? | Yes, with ≥50% headroom | Ratio under 2x |
| Case studies — published with quantified ROI | ≥3 | Zero ROI-quantified |

## How to Use These Numbers
When working on strategy, product, or GTM decisions — check against these metrics. If a proposed action would hurt a leading indicator, flag it. If we're already in red on a metric, all decisions should route through fixing that metric first before adding new work.
