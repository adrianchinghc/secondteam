# The Sovereign Business Filter
*The constitution of Second Team. Every decision passes through all six filters. If it fails any one — we don't do it. No exceptions.*

## The Six Filters

| # | Filter | What It Means |
|---|--------|---------------|
| 01 | **Infinite Scalability** | Revenue must be able to grow 10x without hiring 10x headcount. Reject any model with a labor ceiling. |
| 02 | **Location Independence** | Every function of the business must run from anywhere with internet. No offices, no regional dependencies. |
| 03 | **Asynchronous by Design** | Sales, delivery, and support happen without our real-time presence. The business runs 24/7 while we sleep. |
| 04 | **Low Marginal Delivery Cost** | Each additional customer costs near-zero to serve. No per-client customization, no services drag. |
| 05 | **Leverage Stack** | Code, content, and capital do the work humans would otherwise do. Compounding assets over linear effort. |
| 06 | **AI-Native Workforce** | Our team members are AI agents, not humans. The minimum viable team is 2 founders + N agents. |

## The Core Framing
*"We are building a product company, not a services company. The business must scale like crazy, run while we sleep, be operable from anywhere in the world, and be staffed primarily by AI agents — not humans."*

## What This Filter KILLS
Do not propose or suggest any of these — they are dead:
- DFY consulting with custom scopes (violates filters 1, 3, 4, 6)
- Live cohorts and facilitated programs (violates filters 1, 3)
- Sales calls and human-led selling (violates filters 3, 6)
- Human support teams (violates filter 6)
- Enterprise deals with custom SLAs (violates filters 1, 3, 4)

## Conscious Exceptions
**Beta Phase Onboarding (Phase 3 only):** Founder-assisted onboarding during beta is a conscious and temporary violation of Filter #4 (Low Marginal Delivery Cost). Accepted trade-off: harvest high-quality case studies and activation signal.

**Hard deadline:** By December 31, 2026 all new customer onboarding must be fully self-serve with zero founder touch. After that date, the filter is absolute. No exceptions.

## How to Use This Filter
Before suggesting anything — a feature, a business model, a go-to-market strategy, a partnership — run it through all six filters. If it fails even one, the answer is no. Don't try to find workarounds.

If you're uncertain whether something passes, ask: "Does this require a human to be present, or does it stop scaling if we don't hire more people?" If yes to either → filter violation.
